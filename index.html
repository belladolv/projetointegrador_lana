About

Elizabeth Woolridge Grant

Lana Del Rey, born Elizabeth Woolridge Grant on June 21, 1985, is an American singer-songwriter. Her music is noted for its cinematic quality and exploration of tragic romance, glamour, and melancholia, with frequent references to contemporary pop culture and 1950s–1970s Americana.

She has received numerous accolades throughout her career, including two Brit Awards, two MTV Europe Music Awards, and a Satellite Award, in addition to nominations for six Grammy Awards and a Golden Globe Award.

Del Rey's major label debut, Born to Die (2012), was an international success and spawned the hit single "Summertime Sadness". Her critically acclaimed third album, Ultraviolence (2014), featured greater use of guitar-driven instrumentation and debuted atop the U.S. Billboard 200.
